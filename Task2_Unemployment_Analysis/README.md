📊 Unemployment Analysis with Python
📌 Project Overview

Unemployment is an important economic indicator that reflects the percentage of people in the labor force who are currently without jobs but actively seeking employment. Monitoring unemployment trends helps governments and policymakers understand economic health and labor market conditions.

In this project, I performed data analysis and visualization of unemployment trends in India using Python. The primary goal of the analysis was to explore how unemployment rates changed across different regions and to observe the impact of the COVID-19 pandemic on unemployment rates.

Two datasets related to unemployment in India were used and combined to perform a comprehensive analysis.

🎯 Objective

The main objectives of this project are:

To analyze unemployment rates across different regions in India

To understand unemployment trends over time

To visualize the distribution of unemployment rates

To identify the impact of COVID-19 on unemployment

To compare unemployment rates across urban and rural areas

To explore correlations between different employment-related variables

📂 Dataset Information

Two datasets were used in this project:

1️⃣ Unemployment in India.csv
2️⃣ Unemployment_Rate_upto_11_2020.csv

These datasets contain information about unemployment statistics collected across different regions of India.

Dataset Features

The datasets include the following columns:

Region – State or region in India

Date – Time period when the data was recorded

Frequency – Monthly or periodic data frequency

Estimated Unemployment Rate (%) – Percentage of unemployed people in the labor force

Estimated Employed – Estimated number of employed individuals

Estimated Labour Participation Rate (%) – Percentage of people participating in the labor force

Area – Indicates whether the data is from Urban or Rural areas

📈 Data Visualizations

Several visualizations were created to better understand unemployment trends.

1️⃣ Distribution of Unemployment Rate

A histogram was used to visualize how unemployment rates are distributed across the dataset.

This helps identify whether unemployment rates are concentrated around certain values.

2️⃣ Unemployment Rate by Region

A bar chart was used to compare the average unemployment rate across different regions in India.

This visualization highlights regions with higher unemployment rates.

3️⃣ Unemployment Trend Over Time

A line graph was created to analyze how unemployment rates changed over time.

This helps observe economic trends and fluctuations in unemployment.

4️⃣ Impact of COVID-19

A separate visualization was created to analyze unemployment trends during the COVID-19 pandemic period.

The analysis shows a noticeable increase in unemployment rates during the pandemic.

5️⃣ Correlation Heatmap

A correlation heatmap was used to analyze relationships between numerical variables such as:

unemployment rate

employed population

labor participation rate

6️⃣ Urban vs Rural Unemployment

A comparison was made between Urban and Rural unemployment rates to identify differences in employment trends across different areas.

📊 Key Insights

From the analysis, the following insights were observed:

Unemployment rates varied significantly across different regions in India.

There was a noticeable increase in unemployment during the COVID-19 pandemic.

Some regions experienced higher unemployment compared to others.

Urban and rural areas show different employment patterns.

Data visualization helps identify economic trends more effectively.
